Local Contexts and Maine-eDNA

Documenting Indigenous provenance through sustained partnerships.

Local Contexts Collaboration | Biocultural Notices and Labels

Maine-eDNA is committed to maintaining Indigenous provenance of and future interests in data collected on Indigenous lands and waters. As part of our commitment to sharing and caring for data, Maine-eDNA is working with Local Contexts to openly collaborate with Wabanaki Tribal Nations through the use of Biocultural (BC) Notices and Labels. Maine-eDNA and Local Contexts are collaborating with Wabanaki Tribal Nations to develop BC Notices and Labels projects that also will be shared as models for the international community.

Local Contexts is an international organization founded in 2010 to “enhance and legitimize locally based decision-making and Indigenous governance frameworks for determining ownership, access, and culturally appropriate conditions for sharing historical, contemporary and future collections of cultural heritage and Indigenous data.”
More about Local Contexts

Local Contexts enables the communication of Traditional Knowledge (TK) and Biocultural (BC) Labels and Notices between Indigenous communities and researchers. These human and machine readable digital tags support relationships between Indigenous communities, researchers, and institutions. They help communities, institutions and researchers in the management of intellectual and cultural property, cultural heritage, and environmental data within the digital environment. Traditional Knowledge and Biocultural Notices, applied by a researcher or institution, signal the right of Indigenous communities to define the use of information, collections, and data (including Digital Sequence Information, DSI) generated from biodiversity and genetic resources associated with traditional lands or waters.

As participants in the Maine-eDNA Project, we respectfully acknowledge that we are conducting research on the Homelands of the Penobscot, Passamaquoddy, Maliseet, and Mi’kmaq Wabanaki Tribal Nations. When partnering with Tribal Nations, Maine-eDNA promotes data sharing practices that enable appropriate attribution, appropriate access and appropriate authority. As researchers we are committed to the FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) guiding principles for managing scientific data and stewardship and CARE (Collective Benefit, Authority to Control, Responsibility, Ethics) guiding principles for Indigenous data governance.

Local Contexts Hub | Collaborative Communication

As part of the collaboration between Maine-eDNA and Local Contexts, Maine-eDNA has created a long-term institutional account on the Local Contexts Hub under the Maine Center for Genetics in the Environment (MCGE).

The Local Contexts Hub is a platform for communication and collaboration between communities, institutions, and researchers. Using the Hub, researchers can submit Projects and attach Notices to alert Indigenous communities of research activities, who can then customize and attach provenance, protocol, or permission Labels to that Project to give context on how the data should be used. With the institutional account, researchers aligned with Maine-eDNA do not have to create separate Researcher profiles for Maine-eDNA projects, though profiles may be created for better communication.
